ACM FAccT 2025 – Local Panel

AI and Greece: Interdisciplinary Reflections from Past to Present
Tuesday, June 24 · 9:00 am · Athens Conservatoire

---

Abstract

This panel brings together policy-makers, scholars and NGO representatives in Greece to explore the promises and perils of artificial intelligence. Panelists will offer historical, technical, ethical and social perspectives, reflecting on both the global dimensions of AI and its implications within the Greek context.

---

Organizers

Evaggelia Pitoura

Lead Researcher, Responsible AI Theme, Archimedes – Athena RC; Professor, University of Ioannina

Prof. Pitoura’s research spans responsible AI, graph analytics and data management. She has received multiple best-paper awards, a Marie Curie Fellowship and ACM service honors.

Charalambos Tsekeris

Acting Chair, Greek National Commission for Bioethics & Technoethics; Principal Researcher, Digital Sociology, EKKE

Dr. Tsekeris researches the future of the internet, digital society, and ethical foresight for disruptive technologies, and he serves on Greece’s national AI advisory committees.

---

Participants

Yannis Mastrogeorgiou

Special Secretary of Foresight Strategy, Presidency of the Government

A veteran policy strategist, Mr. Mastrogeorgiou coordinated Greece’s High-Level AI Advisory Committee and previously led communications and think-tank initiatives on AI’s socioeconomic impact.

Lambrini Gyftokosta

Director, AI & Human Rights, Homo Digitalis

Ms. Gyftokosta is a lawyer specialising in data protection and AI ethics with 15 years’ experience, including roles at BEAT, PwC Belgium and EU policy consultations on GDPR and emerging tech.

Kostas Karpouzis

Assistant Professor, Panteion University

Dr. Karpouzis researches affect-adaptive systems and serious games while examining the ethical values embedded in AI, and he sits on Greece’s National Bioethics & Tech Ethics Committee.

Alexandra Mitsotaki

Co-founder & President, World Human Forum

Ms. Mitsotaki leads global initiatives that promote human-centric technology and sustainable development, fostering cross-sector dialogue on AI governance.

Aristotle Tympas

Professor, History & Philosophy of Science, NKUA

Prof. Tympas studies the histories and policies of AI-related technologies and leads EU projects on the ethics of AI and big-data border control.
