ACM FAccT 2025 – Local Panel

AI and Greece: Interdisciplinary Reflections from Past to Present

Tuesday, June 24 • 9:00 am  |  Athens Conservatoire

Abstract

This panel brings together policy makers, scholars and NGO representatives in Greece to explore the promises and perils of artificial intelligence. Panelists will offer historical, technical, ethical and social perspectives, reflecting on both the global dimensions of AI and its implications within the Greek context.

Organizers

Evaggelia Pitoura

Lead Researcher, Responsible AI Theme, Archimedes – Athena RC;
Professor, University of Ioannina, Greece

Prof. Pitoura’s work spans responsible AI and graph analysis. Among her distinctions are best-paper awards, a Marie Curie Fellowship and ACM service awards. She is an ACM Senior Member and serves on multiple national science councils.

Charalambos Tsekeris

Acting Chair, Greek National Commission for Bioethics & Technoethics;
Principal Researcher in Digital Sociology, EKKE

Dr. Tsekeris researches the future of the internet and ethical foresight for disruptive technologies. He serves on Greece’s AI advisory committees and has led the World Internet Project-Greece.

Participants

Yannis Mastrogeorgiou

Special Secretary of Foresight Strategy, Presidency of the Government

A long-time policy strategist, Mr. Mastrogeorgiou coordinated Greece’s High-Level AI Advisory Committee and previously headed media, communications and think-tank initiatives on AI’s socioeconomic impact.

Lambrini Gyftokosta

Director, AI & Human Rights, Homo Digitalis

Ms. Gyftokosta is a lawyer specializing in data protection and AI ethics, with over 15 years’ experience spanning BEAT, PwC Belgium and European policy consultations on GDPR and emerging tech.

Kostas Karpouzis

Assistant Professor, Panteion University

Dr. Karpouzis researches affect-adaptive systems and serious games, while examining the ethical values embedded in AI. He sits on Greece’s National Bioethics & Tech Ethics Committee.

Alexandra Mitsotaki

Co-founder & President, World Human Forum

Aristotle Tympas

Professor, History & Philosophy of Science, NKUA

Prof. Tympas studies the history and policy of AI-related technologies and leads EU projects on the ethics of AI and big-data border control.
