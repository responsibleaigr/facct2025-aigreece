ACM FAccT 2025 – Local Panel

AI and Greece: Interdisciplinary Reflections from Past to Present
Tuesday, June 24 · 9:00 am · Athens Conservatoire

Abstract

This panel brings together policy makers, scholars and NGO representatives in Greece to explore the promises and perils of artificial intelligence. Panelists will offer historical, technical, ethical and social perspectives, reflecting on both the global dimensions of AI and its implications within the Greek context.

Organizers

Evaggelia Pitoura

Lead Researcher, Responsible AI Theme, Archimedes – Athena RC; Professor, University of Ioannina

Prof. Pitoura’s work spans responsible AI and graph analysis. Among her distinctions are best-paper awards, a Marie Curie Fellowship and ACM service awards.

Charalambos Tsekeris

Acting Chair, Greek National Commission for Bioethics & Technoethics; Principal Researcher in Digital Sociology, EKKE

Dr. Tsekeris researches the future of the internet and ethical foresight for disruptive technologies.

Participants

  • Yannis Mastrogeorgiou – Special Secretary of Foresight Strategy, Presidency of the Government
    A long-time policy strategist; coordinated Greece’s High-Level AI Advisory Committee.
  • Lambrini Gyftokosta – Director, AI & Human Rights, Homo Digitalis
    Lawyer specializing in data protection and AI ethics.
  • Kostas Karpouzis – Assistant Professor, Panteion University
    Researches affect-adaptive systems and serious games while examining ethical values embedded in AI.
  • Alexandra Mitsotaki – Co-founder & President, World Human Forum
  • Aristotle Tympas – Professor, History & Philosophy of Science, NKUA
    Studies the history and policy of AI-related technologies and leads EU ethics projects.
